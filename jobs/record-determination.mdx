---
title: 'Record a determination'
description: 'Close a case with a disposition and justification.'
---

A determination is the final call that closes a case: the top-level disposition, the subcategory that narrows *why*, and a short justification future analysts and auditors will read. Saving the determination moves the case to **Resolved** on the queue and locks the record into the audit trail.

<Note>
Cases are not available on Pilot deployments.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the checklist is done">
    Open **RFI / Checklist** and verify every required item is ticked. The Determination tab will not accept a close while required items remain open — resolve them or mark the case **Inconclusive** with a reason.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the Determination tab">
    Switch to **Determination**. The three top-level options sit across the top: **TRUE POSITIVE**, **FALSE POSITIVE**, **INCONCLUSIVE**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pick a disposition and subcategory">
    Select the top-level disposition that matches the evidence, then the subcategory that narrows it. Subcategories include *ATO via SIM swap*, *ATO via phishing*, *Insider threat* (True positive); *Legitimate user*, *Known travel*, *VPN/proxy usage*, *Device change* (False positive); and *Insufficient evidence*, *Awaiting information* (Inconclusive). The subcategory feeds back into Conway's models — be specific.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write a justification and save">
    Write a short paragraph explaining the reasoning and the decisive evidence. Click **SAVE** to close the case. The determination, your reasoning, your name, and the timestamp are written to the audit trail.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Re-opening a determination

Determinations are revisitable. Re-open the case, pick a new disposition, and supply a reason — the change creates a new audit-trail entry alongside the old one rather than overwriting it. Every prior determination remains visible for auditors.

<Tip>
**Consistency check.** Before clicking save, drop the case into chat and ask *"is this determination consistent with how we closed similar cases in the last quarter?"* The assistant surfaces the handful of precedents closest to this one so you can confirm the subcategory fits.
</Tip>
