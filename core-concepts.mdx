Conway carries every run from detection to decision on its own. The sections below define the terms Conway uses within its platform.

## Run

A **run** is one pass of Conway's agent over a table with a stated objective — for example, *"look for unusual card-not-present activity in the last seven days."* You launch runs from the Detection hub. A run produces one or more investigations and ends in `completed` or `failed`.

## Investigation

An **investigation** is one focused look at something the agent surfaced inside a run: a specific account, a cohort, a pattern. Every investigation ends with a verdict and produces a report.

## Report

The **report** is the written output of an investigation — the markdown that explains what was examined, what was found, and why the agent reached its conclusion. The investigation is the UI object; the report is the prose inside it.

## Verdict

Every investigation carries one of three verdicts, analogous to positive, negative, or uncertain.

You can **override** a verdict from the investigation drawer if you disagree with Conway's call. The override flow is documented alongside [Audit a run](/jobs/audit-run), since an audit is usually what justifies one.

## Entity

An **entity** is a real-world thing Conway tracks across runs: a person, an organization, an account, a location, or a **typology** (a recurring behavior pattern, like *ATO via SIM swap*). The same account in two different runs points at the same entity. The **Intel** tab is the library of entities.

## Intelligence

An **intelligence item** is an observation Conway saved about an entity for future runs. Intelligence is how the agent gets smarter about your data over time: a second run that touches the same account benefits from what the first already learned.

## Case

A **case** is the workspace an analyst uses to work a real incident end to end. Where a run is Conway asking *"is there something here?"*, a case is an analyst deciding *"what are we going to do about it?"*. Cases have a status, an SLA, an assigned owner, a determination, and an audit trail. Cases live under **Response**.

<Note>
Cases are not available on Pilot deployments.
</Note>

## Disposition

When you close a case, you record a **disposition** — the final call on whether the activity was real. Three top-level values, each with UI-visible subcategories:

- **True positive** — the activity was of concern and action was warranted.
- **False positive** — the activity turned out to be benign.
- **Inconclusive** — evidence was insufficient to decide either way.

<Note>
Dispositions are not available on Pilot deployments, since Pilots do not have cases to close.
</Note>

## Playbook

A **playbook** is a reusable recipe that fires on a schedule, on a case event, or on demand. Playbooks are configured by the Conway team in Pilot and early full deployments — analysts see their outcomes on cases but do not typically author them.

<Note>
Playbooks are not available on Pilot deployments.
</Note>
