The chat assistant is a pane on the right of every Conway screen. It is the fastest way to understand what you are looking at: drag a run, investigation, entity, rule, or case into chat, then ask your question in plain English.

## How to use the chat

**Attach an artifact.** Drag any card on the page onto the chat pane. A pulsing overlay reads **Drop to attach** while you are over the drop target; release the card and it joins the conversation under **Attached Artifacts** above the message input. You can attach runs, investigations, entities, rules, candidate sets, cases, and reports. Use the **Remove** control to detach.

**Ask a question.** With something attached, type your question. Starter prompts appear above the input as **Try these** pills that change based on the page you are on and the artifact you attached. Use a pill when it matches what you want to ask; otherwise write your own. Specific questions produce specific answers. Answers include **citations** back into the artifact; click a cited phrase to jump to the referenced investigation, entity, or telemetry entry.

**Save or remove intelligence.** Say *"remember that…"* to save an observation about an entity for future runs; *"forget what you remembered about…"* removes it. Both give you a short undo window. See [Manage intelligence](/jobs/manage-intelligence) for the full flow.

## What the assistant can and cannot do

The assistant reads. It can summarize investigations, cross-reference entities against past runs, explain why a verdict landed where it did, and walk you through unfamiliar screens. It does not take state-changing actions on its own. Launching runs, modifying cases, publishing playbooks, and editing sources all happen in the UI.

Two exceptions: the **Start a run** suggestion opens the draft-run dialog for you (you still confirm before launching), and you can create or remove **intelligence** about an entity by asking.

<Tip>
**When in doubt, ask the chat.** Any time a screen, a verdict, or a counter doesn't make sense, drop what you are looking at into the pane and ask. Job pages throughout this guide surface specific prompts as inline `<Tip>` callouts where chat is genuinely faster than the manual flow.
</Tip>
